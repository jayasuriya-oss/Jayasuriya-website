Academic & Strategic Research

Retailers’ Willingness to Stock Upcycled Food Products in Coimbatore City

Master's Seminar Research

Research Objective

To evaluate the strategic and operational determinants governing the structural adoption of circular economy food products in standard retail environments, converting subjective willingness into actionable S-O-R (Stimulus-Organism-Response) models.

Key Constructs Studied

• Commercial Attractiveness (Profitability & Margin Potential)
• Operational Feasibility (Supply Chain & Shelf Life)
• Perceived Consumer Demand & Value Proposition

Strategic Implications & Industry Relevance

The findings bridge the critical gap between sustainable food production and actual commercial retail adoption. By providing hard data on the factors that drive shelf-space allocation for upcycled goods, the research delivers an immediate, data-backed roadmap for sustainable food enterprises to structure their B2B pitches, adjust pricing strategies, and seamlessly integrate into modern retail chains.

Agroecology And Economic Sustainability

Stella International Publication · Apr 1, 2025

This chapter explores the role of agroecology in promoting economic sustainability in farming. It highlights key indicators such as profitability, resource efficiency, risk management, and long-term viability. Agroecology offers solutions like reduced input costs, better market access, and resilience through diversified farming. The chapter also discusses supportive policies, technological innovations, and challenges in adoption. Ultimately, it emphasizes that integrating traditional practices with modern tools can create sustainable, profitable, and resilient agricultural systems for the future.

Powdery Mildew Infection in Wild Poinsettia (Euphorbia heterophylla Linn.)

Greenaria – An Agricultural Monthly e-Magazine · Oct 25, 2024

This study focuses on the identification and ecological impact of powdery mildew infection in Euphorbia heterophylla, a common weed in soybean, bean, and corn fields. The infection was first observed in November 2023 at the Agricultural College and Research Institute, Vazhavachanur. Morphological and molecular tools were used to confirm the pathogen identity. The study also examined environmental factors such as temperature, humidity, and plant density influencing disease development. Curious how a common weed can reveal big lessons in plant health?
